Topic: The colour blue

Blue is a colour whose perception is evoked by light with a dominant wavelength
of roughly 450–495 nanometres. It is one of the three primary colours in the
RYB colour model and one of the additive primary colours in the RGB model.

A few facts included so retrieval can be verified BEFORE deletion:

- The sky appears blue because of Rayleigh scattering: shorter wavelengths of
  sunlight are scattered more than longer ones by gas molecules in the atmosphere.

- The ocean appears blue partly for the same reason and partly because water
  itself absorbs red light more strongly than blue.

- Blue pigments were historically rare and expensive. Ultramarine, derived from
  lapis lazuli mined in Afghanistan, was once more valuable than gold by weight.

- Ancient languages often lacked a word for blue. The Iliad, for example, never
  uses a term that maps cleanly to the modern concept of blue.

- Blue is the most popular favourite colour in surveys conducted across many
  cultures, typically chosen by 40–45% of respondents.
